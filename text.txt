Kaladin felt good.

Not great. Not after spending weeks hiding in an occupied city. Not after driving himself to physical and
emotional exhaustion. Not after what had happened to Teft.

He stood at his window on the first moring of the month. Sunlight streamed into the room around him,
wind tickling his hair. He shouldn't have felt good. Yes, he'd helped protect Urithiru-but that victory had
come at an agonizing cost. Beyond that, Dalinar had made a deal with the enemy: in just ten days, the
champion of Honor and the champion of Odium would decide the fate of all Roshar.
